# Data README: Pollination Experiments 2025

**Created by:** Ferne Kotlyar \| **Date created:** October 20, 2025

**Date range of data collection:** March 2025 - September 2025

Pollination experiments were conducted in March and April 2025, fruits were monitored from April to August 2025. Field work was conducted at the Las Cruces Biological Station in Costa Rica and collected by Ferne Kotlyar and Michael Atencio.

## Raw Data Files

- **PRM_2025.csv:** Pollination data taken by Ferne Kotlyar in March 2025 which includes the pollination data of flowers (treatments, pollination dates, bracts, flower locations, etc.) for the main experiment.
- **Fruit_Set_2025.csv:** Fruit set data collected from the PRM 2025 experiment (datasheet above) taken primarily by Michael (Mike) Atencio in April - July 2025.
- **Self_Nectar_Pollination.csv:** Pollination data for the self-Nectar experiment with 2 treatments (self-nectar and open pollination control) taken by Michael Atencio in April & May 2025.
- Self_Nectar_Fruit_Set.csv: Fruit set data collected from the self nectar experiment (datasheet above) taken by Michael in June & July 2025.

## Background Information

The 2024 field season showed us that flowers were able to produce fruit after being treated with simulated visits (nectar drop). In 2025, our goal was to determine what about this treatment was working - was nectar alone or bill contact alone sufficient to reproduce the results?

## Treatments

Previously bagged *Heliconia tortuosa* flowers were pollinated using 5 main treatments:

- **Open Pollination:** Flowers were left open (not bagged) and unmanipulated (no hand pollination) for wild pollinators to visit.

- **Nectar Drop/Simulated Visit:** Nectar from *H. tortuosa* flowers at least 100 m away was placed on the stigma of target flowers. A bill replica was inserted into a flower to collect nectar and wiped off on the stigma, but was not inserted into the flower.

  - **Self-nectar:** To ensure that the type of nectar (self vs. outcrossed) did not make a difference, later in the season, we ran a separate experiment with self nectar that came from another flower on the same inflorescence.

- **Nectar:** After hand pollination with far pollen, nectar from a different far flower was deposited directly on the stigma by a 3D printed resin bill. Nothing was inserted into the flower. This was followed up with another "self-nectar" treatment in April that was identical to the nectar treatment, only instead of using far nectar, we used self nectar from another flower of the same inflorescence.

- **Sugar Water:** Same as above, but sugar water at the same concentration as *H. tortuosa* nectar.

- **No Nectar/Bill Insertion:** After hand pollination with far pollen, the stigma of the mother flower was lifted up and a 3D printed resin bill was inserted into the flower using the same technique as in Simulated Visit, only under the stigma, making sure that nectar did not land on the stigmatic surface.

## Data Collection Methods

*Heliconia tortuosa* flowers were first hand pollinated, then one of the 5 treamtents above was applied. Flowers were marked and applied by bract to avoid over-manipulation of the relatively delicate flowers. After hand pollinations were complete, flowers were monitored every 2 weeks until all fruits either matured (turned blue) and were collected or were aborted (shrivelled up and died). Every 2 weeks, abortion data was collected to keep tabs on which fruits were growing and which had already aborted.

Once all fruits were collected, they were measured in weight (g) and diamter (mm), then the seeds were counted (*H. tortuosa* fruits can produce a maximum of 3 seeds) and weighed (g).

**Note:** There were some notable differences between 2024 and 2025 data collection methods.

- In 2024, we used a filament 3D printed bill replica; whereas in 2025, we used a resin 3D printed bill replica.
- In 2024, we killed flowers that could not be pollinated a day later using nail polish; whereas in 2025, we simply removed the immature fruit.
