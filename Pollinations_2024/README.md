# Pollination Experiments 2024 README

**Pollination Experiments: Simulated Visits.** Pollination experiments from 2024.

Pollination experiments were conducted in March and April 2024, fruits were monitored from April to September 2024. Field work was conducted at the Las Cruces Biological Station in Costa Rica.

## Background Information

While the phenomenon of "pollination recognition" was reproducible, the previsouly proposed mechansim of nectar extraction by Betts et al., 2015, was not reproducible. See the "Pollinator Recognition in Heliconia" section for further detail. The goal of this data section was to test a new mechanism for pollinator recognition and compare it to hand pollination alone and nectar extraction. The new mechanism is called "simulated visit", where we use a 3D printed green hermit hummingbird bill replica to simulate or mimick a traplining hummingbird visit to an *H. tortuosa* flower. For further details about how the hummingbird behaviour was replicated, see the "Nectar Dye" section.

## Treatments

*Heliconia tortuosa* flowers were pollinated using 3 main treatments:

- **Hand Pollination:** Simple application of pollen from far flowers (more than 100 m away) onto the stigmas of target flowers (originally called "Hand Pollination Only")

- **Nectar Extraction:** Hand pollination (as above), then nectar extraction.

- **Nectar Drop/Simulated Visit:** Hand pollination (as above), insertion of female green hermit bill replica into pollen donor flower, then insertion and extraction of the same bill replica into the target flower. This was meant to replicate traplining hummingbird behaviour as we see in slow motion videos. In the manuscript, this was renamed to "Simulated Visit" to increase intuitive understanding of the treatment.
