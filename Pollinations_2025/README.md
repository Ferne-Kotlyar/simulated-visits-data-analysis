# Pollination Experiments 2025 README

**Pollination Experiments: Simulated Visit Mechanism.** Pollination experiments from 2025.

Main pollination experiments were conducted in March 2025. Further pollination experiments (two treatments: open pollination and self-nectar) were conducted in April 2025. Fruits were monitored from April to September 2025. Field work was conducted at the Las Cruces Biological Station in Costa Rica.

## Background Information

Given that the simulated visits (previously called Nectar Drop) treatment produced significantly more fruit than nectar extraction or hand pollination in the 2024 pollination experiments, in this set of pollination experiments, our goal was to uncover the mechanism behind this successful treatment. We compared two possible mechanisms: nectar or sugar water deposition directly onto the stigma (without bill insertion) or bill insertion alone without nectar deposition. We compared these possible mechanisms against an open control and against simulated visits.

## Treatments

*Heliconia tortuosa* flowers were pollinated using 5 main treatments:

- **Open Pollination:** Flowers were left open and unmanipulated for wild pollinators to visit.

- **Nectar Drop/Simulated Visit:** Hand pollination with far (more than 100 m away) pollen, insertion of female green hermit bill replica into pollen donor flower, then insertion and extraction of the same bill replica into the target flower. This was meant to replicate traplining hummingbird behaviour as we see in slow motion videos. Note: the only difference from the 2024 version of this treatment was the material of the 3D printed bill - in 2024, we used a 3D printed filament bill, whereas in 2025, we used a 3D printed resin bill (for all treatments in 2025).

- **Nectar:** After hand pollination with far pollen, nectar from a different far flower was deposited directly on the stigma by a 3D printed resin bill. Nothing was inserted into the flower. This was followed up with another "self-nectar" treatment in April/May that was identical to the nectar treatment, only instead of using far nectar, we used self nectar from another flower of the same inflorescence.

- **Sugar Water:** Same as above, but sugar water at the same concentration as *H. tortuosa* nectar.

- **No Nectar:** After hand pollination with far pollen, the stigma of the mother flower was lifted up and a 3D printed resin bill was inserted into the flower using the same technique as in Simulated Visit, only under the stigma, making sure that nectar did not land on the stigmatic surface.
