# Data README: Pollination Experiments 2024

Pollination Experiments: Simulated Visits.

**Created by:** Ferne Kotlyar \| **Date created:** October 20, 2025

**Updated by:** Ferne Kotlyar \| **Date updated:** May 07, 2026

**Date range of data collection:** March 2024 - September 2024

Pollination experiments were conducted in March and April 2024, fruits were monitored from April to September 2024. Field work was conducted at the Las Cruces Biological Station in Costa Rica and collected by Ferne Kotlyar and Michael Atencio-Picado.

## Raw Data Files

- **Fruit_Set_2024.csv:** Fruit set data taken by Michael Atencio-Picado from May-August 2024. It includes data about only the flowers that matured into fruits (fruit weight, number of seeds, treatment, etc.) from all the pollination experiments conducted in 2024.

- **PRM_Trials.csv:** Field data taken by Ferne Kotlyar in March 2024, which includes the pollination data of flowers (treatments, pollination dates, bracts, flower locations, etc.).

- **PRM_Extended.csv:** Field data taken by Michael Atencio-Picado in April 2024, which same structure as in "PRM_Trials.csv", but with the ND (nectar drop) treatment split into 3 different types of pollen: self, near-neighour and far. In PRM_Trials.csv, only far pollen is used.

## Treatments

*Heliconia tortuosa* flowers that were previously bagged were pollinated using 3 treatments that were retained for this analysis:

- **Hand Pollination:** Simple application of pollen from far flowers (more than 100 m away) onto the stigmas of target flowers (previously called "Hand Pollination Only").

- **Nectar Extraction:** Hand pollination (as above), then nectar extraction.

- **Nectar Drop/Simulated Visit:** Hand pollination (as above), insertion of female green hermit bill replica into pollen donor flower, then insertion and extraction of the same bill replica into the target flower. This was meant to replicate traplining hummingbird behaviour as we see in slow motion videos. In the manuscript, this was renamed to "Simulated Visit" to increase intuitive understanding of the treatment. We only used far pollen to pollinate all flowers instances where we used near-neighbour or self-pollen were filtered out.

## Data Collection Methods

*Heliconia tortuosa* flowers were first hand pollinated, then one of the 3 treatments above was applied. Flowers were marked and applied by bract to avoid over-manipulation of the relatively delicate flowers. After hand pollinations were complete, flowers were monitored every 2 weeks until all fruits either matured (turned blue) and were collected or were aborted (shriveled up and died). Every 2 weeks, abortion data was collected to keep tabs on which fruits were growing and which had already aborted.

Once all fruits were collected, they were measured in weight (g) and diameter (mm), then the seeds were counted (*H. tortuosa* fruits can produce a maximum of 3 seeds) and weighed (g).
